Percy Jackson by Rick Riordan

The Lightning Thief by Rick Riordan is set in a modern world where ancient Greek gods are real and still

influence human life. The story follows Percy Jackson, a twelve-year-old boy who discovers he is a

demigod—the son of Poseidon. After being accused of stealing Zeus’s lightning bolt, Percy is sent on a quest with

his friends Annabeth and Grover to recover the stolen weapon, clear his name, and prevent a war among the gods.

CHRISTIAN: NO

AGE: 13+; Contains polytheism, disrespect for authority, and violence.

Harry Potter by J.K.Rowling

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone by J.K. Rowling follows Harry, an orphan who learns on his eleventh

birthday that he’s a wizard. At Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, he makes friends, discovers

his past, and uncovers a plot to steal a powerful magical object—all while facing the dark legacy of the

villain who killed his parents.

CHRISTIAN: NO, but does showcase many Christian topics

AGE: 13+; Contains violence and magic

Keeper of the Lost Cities by Shannon Messanger

Keeper of the Lost Cities follows Sophie Foster, a twelve-year-old girl with telepathic abilities who

discovers she’s an elf from a hidden world. As she leaves her human life behind, Sophie uncovers secrets

about her past and her powerful abilities, becoming the key to a dangerous mystery that could change

both worlds.

CHRISTIAN: NO

AGE: 12+; Contains magic, rebellion, and violence
