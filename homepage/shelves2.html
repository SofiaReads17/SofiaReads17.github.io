Sunrise on the Reaping by Suzanne Collins

Sunrise on the Reaping is a prequel novel by Suzanne Collins featuring a young Haymitch Abernathy as he

faces the horrors of the 50th Hunger Games. Chosen for the Quarter Quell, Haymitch must survive

against doubled odds and a Capitol that wants him broken. His sharp mind and stubborn will make him

stand out, even as he mourns the life and love he’s been forced to leave behind.

CHRISTIAN: No

AGE: 13+; Contains violence and sensitive topics

Diary of a Wimpy Kid: Party-pooper by Jeff Kinney

Diary of a Wimpy Kid: Partypooper is a book by Jeff Kinney featuring Greg Heffley, a middle schooler with

big plans for his birthday. When his family forgets the special day, Greg takes matters into his own hands

and throws an extravagant party to get the attention he craves. Greg’s mix of confidence and

cluelessness makes him as entertaining as ever, as he fumbles through friendship, family, and growing up.

CHRISTIAN: No, but it does talk about Greg and his family going to church.

AGE: 9+; Contains immature humor

Percy Jackson and the Triple-headed Godess by Rick Riordan

Percy Jackson and the Olympians: Wrath of the Triple Goddess is a novel by Rick Riordan featuring Percy

in his senior year of high school. Tasked by the goddess Hecate to watch over her magical pets, Percy’s

plans unravel when Grover’s mistake leads to chaos. The trio races to fix the mess before Hecate returns.

With humor, heart, and mythological mayhem, Percy’s journey shows the strength of friendship and growth.

CHRISTIAN: No

AGE: 12+; Contains polytheism, disrespect for authority, magic, and violence
